<div class="group">
<p>U.S. stock futures&nbsp;rose Wednesday, with Google parent&nbsp;Alphabet&rsquo;s over 9% premarket surge on strong earnings pushing Nasdaq futures more than 1.5% higher as Wall Street looks to advance for the fourth straight session. Shares of Facebook parent&nbsp;Meta Platforms&nbsp;rose 3% in the premarket ahead of earnings out after the bell.The late-January market rally&nbsp;continued into February, with the&nbsp;Dow Jones Industrial Average, the&nbsp;S&amp;P 500&nbsp;and the&nbsp;Nasdaq&nbsp;closing up roughly 0.7% each. The Nasdaq remained in correction territory heading into the new trading day.</p>
<div class="BoxInline-container  ">
<div id="BoxInline-ArticleBody-6" class="BoxInline-container" data-module="mps-slot"></div>
</div>
<p>Ahead of Friday&rsquo;s monthly employment report, ADP said before-the-bell Wednesday that&nbsp;U.S. companies cut 301,000 jobs&nbsp;in January as the spread of the Covid omicron variant slammed hiring. Economists had expected growth of 200,000 new private-sector positions. December was downwardly revised to a 776,000 gain. It&rsquo;s worth noting that during the pandemic, the ADP numbers have not been the best indicator of the government&rsquo;s overall jobs report.</p>

<p>If Alphabet&rsquo;s premarket advance were to hold at the open, the stock would top November&rsquo;s all-time high and go back above a $2 trillion market value. Late Tuesday, the tech giant reported better-than-expected earnings and revenue for the fourth quarter.&nbsp;It beat estimates&nbsp;in the services and cloud businesses. Alphabet&nbsp;announced a 20-for-1 stock split, pending shareholder approval, leading to speculation about whether the name could eventually be added to the Dow 30. It also repurchased a record $13.47 billion of stock in the quarter.</p>
